How to use the app

Matches

  • All 104 matches with real dates, venues and kick-off times (your local time zone).
  • Every unplayed match shows a prediction: Home / Draw / Away probabilities and the most likely score.
  • When a match finishes, press ⟳ Sync results from web to pull real scores — or type the score in yourself. Both do exactly the same thing (sync overwrites manual entries once the official score is in the feed).
  • If a knockout match ends in a draw, pick the penalty shoot-out winner with the buttons under the match.

Groups

Live tables of all 12 groups, recalculated on every result. Green — direct qualification (top 2), blue — current 3rd place (the 8 best third-placed teams also advance).

Knockout

The official FIFA bracket from the Round of 32 to the final. It fills in automatically as group standings are decided, including the allocation of third-placed teams to their bracket slots.

Team chances

Press ▶ Recalculate and the app simulates the entire remaining tournament thousands of times, then shows every team's probability of reaching each stage — all the way to lifting the trophy.

Ratings

Each team's Elo strength. Starting values are editable — if you think the model underrates someone, change the number and all predictions update instantly. Reset all wipes your results and edits.

How the predictions work

  1. Elo ratings. Every team starts with a strength rating (≈ eloratings.net at the start of the tournament). Each result updates it: beating a strong team earns many points, beating a weak one few; bigger margins move ratings more (K=50 with a goal-difference multiplier). Mexico, the USA and Canada get a +80 home bonus in their own country. This is how the app learns from results — a team on a surprise run climbs the ratings and its future predictions sharpen match by match.
  2. Poisson score model. The Elo gap in a match converts into expected goals for each side; a Poisson distribution over all scorelines gives the Home/Draw/Away percentages and the likely score on each match card.
  3. Monte Carlo simulation. The whole remaining tournament is played out 2,000–10,000 times: unplayed matches are randomly “rolled” from their Poisson distributions, group tables use FIFA tiebreakers, third-placed teams go into the real bracket, knockout draws are settled as shoot-outs, and Elo keeps updating inside every simulation. How often a team reaches a stage across all runs = its probability.

Matches you've already entered are treated as facts — only the future is simulated. The more results you feed in, the sharper the forecasts.

Good to know

  • Your data is local. Results and rating edits live in your browser (localStorage). A different device or browser starts fresh; nobody else sees your data.
  • Sync needs internet and uses public proxies — if it fails, paste the feed JSON via “Import JSON” or type scores manually.
  • Penalty winners aren't in the feed — after syncing a drawn knockout match, pick who went through by hand.
  • It's a model, not a crystal ball 🙂 — probabilities are calibrated guesses from team strength, not inside info on lineups or injuries.
